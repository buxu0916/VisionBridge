Visually-Impaired-Friendly Places
Posted by BalaBala ¬∑ 1h ago
During last school holiday, I visited two public places in Malaysia that are surprisingly accessible for people with visual impairments: IKEA Cheras and MyTown KL.

IKEA had tactile paths on the floor and the staff were super helpful. I asked for assistance and they guided me around the product displays. The product descriptions are mostly visual, but they offered verbal explanations.

At MyTown, the lifts have Braille and voice assistance. There are also staff at the concierge who were trained to assist OKU customers.

Do you know more places that are blind-friendly in Klang Valley or anywhere in Malaysia? Share here!
